Japchae

Ingredients

  • 8oz Kelp Noodles
  • 1 Package of Seafood Mushrooms (Roots Cut Off and Pulled Apart)
  • Roasted Sesame Oil
  • 8oz Chuck Roast (Sliced Thinly Against the Grain)
  • 8oz Shrimp Peeled
  • 4 Cloves Minced Garlic
  • 4 Tablespoons of Soy Sauce
  • 1 Onion Sliced
  • 4 Green Onions Sliced Thinly
  • 2 Eggs
  • 1 Tablespoon Erythritol (or sweetener of choice)
  • 1/2 Teaspoon of Pepper
  • 2 Tablespoons of Baking Soda
  • Pinch of Sesame Seeds (Optional)

Steps

  1. Mix 2 tablespoons of soy sauce, 2 cloves of minced garlic, 1 tablespoon of erythritol, and 1/4 teaspoon of pepper. Add chuck roast and mix thoroughly. Set aside to marinate.
  2. Bring to a boil a pot of water. While it's heating up, place kelp noodles into large heat proof container. Add 2 tablespoons of baking soda. Then add boiling water to the bowl and stir. Let sit. This will soften the noodles. Set aside for now.
  3. In a wok or large skillet on high heat, add a little bit of oil. Let heat up then add 2 scrambled eggs. Let cook then flip. Once done, remove from heat and slice into thin ribbons. Place into a large bowl.
  4. In the same wok or skillet, add a bit more oil. Fry onions until soft then add the rest of the garlic. Fry until fragrant then remove and add to bowl of cooked ingredients.
  5. Fry the mushrooms until all the water is released and evaporated. Once mushrooms brown a bit, remove and add to the bowl.
  6. Stir fry the beef and shrimp together. Cook in small batches to avoid overcrowding the pan. Remove from heat and add to the bowl.
  7. Drain the noodles into a collander and rinse with water. Shake to dry off as much water as possible. Add to bowl of cooked ingredients.
  8. Mix 2 tablespoons of soy sauce, 2 cloves of minced garlic, 1 tablespoon of erythritol, and 1/4 teaspoon of pepper. Add to the bowl of ingredients and mix.
  9. Stir fry all cooked ingredients together one more time to heat up. Add additional servings of the sauce if needed in the ratios of the previous step.
  10. Serve with a pinch of sliced green onions and sesame seeds. Enjoy!
